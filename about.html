About Autumn Doshier

Therapy rooted in respect, insight, and whole-person care.

I am a Licensed Professional Counselor with a master's degree in Clinical Mental Health Counseling from Southern Oregon University. I specialize in individual therapy and maintain a private practice in Southern Oregon.

My professional background includes work across psychiatric care, universities and schools, community integrative health, crisis services, outpatient mental health, and private practice. That range of experience supports a counseling style that is both grounded and adaptable.

Mission and guiding values

My work is centered on honoring each client as unique and respecting the personal process of development, healing, and discovery.

I believe people hold deep wisdom about what helps them find meaning, fulfillment, and a more connected life.

Driving goals in the therapeutic process

Individualized care

I shape therapy around each client's needs, goals, pace, and lived experience rather than relying on a one-size-fits-all formula.

Healing and empowerment

My practice is guided by a social justice and anti-oppressive framework that values equity, inclusion, and client empowerment in the healing process.

Specialty work

I often work with clients who have been impacted by trauma and have specialized training in trauma-specific models of care for both acute and longer-term recovery support.

  • Trauma and trauma recovery support
  • Grief and loss
  • Anxiety and depression
  • Stress management and self-compassion
  • Disordered eating concerns
  • Perinatal and postpartum mood disorders
  • Self-esteem and self-worth
  • Gender, sexuality, and life transitions

Types of therapy offered

My therapeutic approach is eclectic and integrative, drawing from methods that support the whole person and remain rooted in each client's goals.

  • Humanistic and relational therapy
  • Attachment-focused and trauma-informed care
  • Interpersonal neurobiology
  • Polyvagal-informed support
  • EMDR
  • Brainspotting
  • Somatic interventions and resourcing techniques
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)
  • Expressive Arts Therapy
  • HAES and Intuitive Eating-informed support

A little beyond the office

Outside of work, I enjoy good humor, gardening, hiking, board games, and spending time with family. That same sense of warmth and groundedness carries into the counseling relationship.
